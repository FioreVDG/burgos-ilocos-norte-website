Office of the Mayor

The Local Chief Executive heads the office of the Mayor. It is composed of the following sections: Personal Staff, Civil Security Office, Human Resource Management Office, Administrative Section, Local Disaster and Risk Reduction Office, Library Section, Economic Enterprise Management and Slaughterhouse Section, and General Services.

The office of the Mayor through the Local Chief Executive exercises such powers and performs such duties and functions as provided for under the New Local Government Code and other applicable laws. To enumerate some of the basics functions this office.

  • Exercises general supervision and over all programs, projects, services and activities of the Local Government Unit;
  • Enforces all laws and ordinances relative to the governance of the municipality and the exercise of its corporate powers, and implement all approved policies, programs, projects, services and activities of the municipality;
  • Initiates and maximizes the generation of resources and revenues and apply the same to the implementation of the development plans, program objectives and priorities as provided under the New Local Government Code of 1991, particularly those resources and revenues programmed for agro-industrial development and countrywide growth and progress;
  • Ensures the delivery of the basic services and the provision of adequate facilities; and,
  • Exercises such other powers and performs such other duties and functions as may be prescribed by laws or ordinances.
  • The Office of the Mayor through its different sections provides other services as follows: library services, market and slaughterhouse services, administrative services, general utility services, security services and social welfare services.

Office of the Sangguniang Bayan

The office of the Sangguniang Bayan is headed by the Municipal Vice Mayor as the Presiding Officer and is composed of the Sangguniang Bayan Members, the Liga ng mga Barangay (LMB) President and the Sangguniang Kabataan (SK) Federated President. The secretariat is headed by the Sangguniang Bayan Secretary and has three (3) Casual Employees.

The Sangguniang Bayan as the legislative body of the municipality enacts ordinances, approves resolutions and appropriates funds for the general welfare of the municipality and its constituents pursuant to Section 16 of the Local Government Code and in the proper exercise of the corporate powers of the municipality as provided for under Section 22 of the Local Government Code. Major functions of the Sangguniang Bayan from which other related functions emanate are:

  • Approves ordinances and pass resolutions necessary for an efficient and effective Municipal Government;
  • Generates and maximizes the use of resources and revenues for the development plans, program objectives and priorities of the municipality as provided for under Section 18 of the Local Government Code with particular attention to agro-industrial development and countrywide growth progress;
  • Subject to the provisions of Book II of the Local Government Code, grants franchise, enacts ordinances authorizing the issuance of permits or licenses, or enacts ordinances levying taxes, fees and charges upon such conditions and for such purposes intended to promote the general welfare of the inhabitants of the municipality;
  • Regulates activities relative to the use of land, buildings and structures within the municipality in order to promote the general welfare;
  • Approves ordinances which shall ensure the efficient and effective delivery of basic services and facilities as provided for under Section 17 of the Local Government Code; and,
  • Exercises such other powers and performs such other duties and functions as may be prescribed by laws and ordinances.

The Vice Mayor in his official capacity as such is the Presiding Office of the Sangguniang Bayan and signs all warrants drawn on the Municipal Treasury for all expenditures appropriated for the operation of the Sangguniang Bayan; appoints all officials and employees of the Sangguniang Bayan in accordance with civil service laws, rules and regulations except those who manner of appointment is specifically provided in the Government Code; assumes the Office of the Municipal Mayor for the unexpired term of the latter in the event of permanent disability; exercises the powers and performs the duties and functions of the Municipal Mayor in cases of temporary vacancy; and exercises such other powers and performs such other duties and functions as may be prescribed by law or ordinance.

On the other hand, the Sangguniang Bayan Secretary who heads the Secretariat services attends meeting of the SB and keeps a journal of their proceedings; keeps the Seal of the Local Government Unit and affixes the same with his/her signature to all ordinances, resolutions and other official acts of the SB and presents the same to the Presiding Officer for his signature; forwards to the Mayor for approval copies of ordinances enacted by the Sangguniang Bayan and duly certified by the Presiding Officer; forwards to the Sangguniang Bayan copies of approved Sangguniang Barangay ordinances and to the Sangguniang Panlalawigan of approved Sangguniang Bayan ordinances; furnishes upon request of any interested party, certified copies of records of public character in his custody, upon payment to the Treasurer of such fees as may be prescribed by ordinance; records in a book kept for the purpose, all ordinances and resolutions enacted or adopted by the Sanggunian, with the dates of passage and publication thereof; keeps his office and all non-confidential records therein open to the public during the usual business hours; translate into the dialect used by the majority of the inhabitants all ordinances and resolutions immediately after their approval, and causes the publication of the same together with the original version in the manner provided under the Government Code; takes the custody of the local archives and annually accounts for the same: and exercises such other powers and performs such other duties and functions as may be prescribed by law or ordinance relative to his position.

Office of the Municipal Treasury

The Municipal Treasurer heads the Office of the Municipal Treasury. He is assisted by the Assistant Municipal Treasurer in the delivery of the Administrative functions. This Office is composed of a Revenue Collection Officer I, Revenue Collection Clerk II, Revenue Collection Clerk I and a Casual-Clerk I.

The Municipal Treasurer takes charge of the Treasury office and performs the duties and functions as provided for under the New Local Government Code such as; advise the Mayor, the Sangguniang Bayan and other Local Government and National Offices concerned regarding disposition of the local government funds and on such other matters relative to public finance; take custody of and exercise proper management of the funds of the Local Government Unit; takes charge of the disbursement of all local government funds and such other funds the custody of which may be entrusted to him by law or other competent authority; inspects private commercial and industrial establishments within the jurisdiction of the Local Government Unit in relation to the implementation of Tax Ordinances; maintains and updates the Tax Information System of the Local Government Unit and exercises such other powers and performs such other duties and functions as may be prescribed by law or ordinance.

On the other hand, the Assistant Municipal Treasurer shall assist the Municipal Treasurer and perform such duties as the latter may assign to him. He shall have authority to administer oaths concerning notices and notifications to those delinquent in the payment of the real property tax and concerning official matters relating to the accounts of the Treasurer or otherwise arising in the Offices of the Treasurer and the Assessor.

Office of the Municipal Assessor

This Office is headed by the Municipal Assessor. It has two (2) sections: the Tax Mapping Section and the Assessment Section. Under the Tax Mapping Section, there is a Mapper I while there are three (3) Assessment Clerks under the Assessment Section.

The Municipal Assessor takes charge of the Assessor’s office and performs the duties provided for under Book II of the Government Code such as; ensures that all laws and policies governing the appraisal and assessment of real properties for taxation purposes are properly executed; initiates, reviews and recommends changes in policies and objectives, plans and programs, techniques, procedures and practices in the valuation and assessment of real properties for taxation purposes; establishes a systematic method of real property assessment; installs and maintains a real property identification and accounting system; prepares, installs and maintains a system of Tax mapping, showing graphically all properties subject to assessment and gather all data concerning the same; conducts frequent physical surveys to verify and determine whether all real properties within the municipality are properly listed in the assessment rolls; exercises the functions of appraisal and assessment primarily for taxation purposes of all real properties in the Local Government Unit; prepares a schedule of the fair market value for the different classes of real properties; issues, upon request of any interested party, certified copies of assessment records of real property and all other records relative to its assessment, upon payment of a service charge or fee to the treasurer; submits every semester a report of all assessments, as well as cancellations and modifications of assessments to the Local Chief Executive and the Sanggunian; attends personally or through an authorized representative, all sessions of the Local Board of Assessment Appeals whenever his assessment is the subject of the appeal, and presents or submits any information or record in his possession as may be required by the board; and exercises such other powers and performs such other duties and functions as may be prescribed by law or ordinance.

Office of the Municipal Accountant

The Municipal Accountant assisted by an Accountant II, Accountant I and an Accounting Clerk III, is manning this Office. The Municipal Accountant is in charge of both the accounting and internal audit services of the Local Government Unit.

The Municipal Accountant as provided for by the New Local Government Code installs and maintains an internal audit system in the Local Government Unit; prepares and submits financial statements to the mayor and the Sangguniang Bayan; appraises the Sanggunian and other local government official on the financial condition and operations of the Local Government Unit; certifies to the availability of budgetary allotment to which expenditures and obligations may be properly charged; reviews supporting documents before preparation of vouchers to determine completeness of requirements; prepares statement of cash advances, liquidation, salaries, allowances, reimbursements and remittances pertaining to the Local Government Unit; prepares statements of journal vouchers and liquidation of the same and other adjustments related thereto; posts individual disbursements to the subsidiary ledgers, maintains individual ledgers for officials and employees of the Local Government Unit pertaining to payrolls and deductions; records and posts data of purchased furniture, fixtures and equipment, including disposal thereof, if any; accounts for all issued requests for obligation and maintains and keeps all records and reports related thereof; prepares journals and the analysis of obligations and maintains and keeps all records and reports related thereto; and exercises such other powers and performs such other duties and functions as may be provided by law or ordinance.

Office of the Municipal Budget Officer

The Office is headed by the Municipal Budget Officer and is supported by the Budget Officer II.

The Municipal Budget Officer prepares forms, orders and circulars embodying instructions on budgetary and appropriation matters for the signature of the Mayor; reviews and consolidated the budget proposals of different departments and offices of the Local Government Unit; assists the Mayor in the preparation of the budget and during budget hearings; studies and evaluates budgetary implications of proposed legislation and submits comments and recommendations thereon; submits periodic budgetary reports to the Department of Budget and Management (DBM); coordinates with the Treasurer, the Accountant and the Planning and Development Coordinator for the purpose of budgeting; assists the Sanggunian in reviewing the approved budgets of barangays; coordinates with the Planning Development Coordinator in the formulation of the Development plan; and exercises such other powers and performs such other duties and functions as may be prescribed by law or ordinance.

Office of the Municipal Planning and Development Coordinator

This Office is headed by the Municipal Planning and Development Coordinator (MPDC). It is composed of the aforementioned MPDC, Project Development Officer II, and Project Development Assistant.

The Municipal Planning and Development Coordinator (MPDC) is responsible in the discharge of duties and functions as provided by the New Local Government Code such as the formulation of integrated economic, social, physical and other development plans and policies for consideration of the Municipal Development Council (MDC). The Office integrates and coordinates all sectoral plans and studies undertaken by the different functional groups or agencies. In addition, the Office monitors and evaluates the implementation of the different plans, programs, projects and activities in the LGU in accordance with the approved development plan; prepares comprehensive plans and other development planning documents for the consideration of the Municipal Development Council; analyzes the income ad expenditures patterns and formulates and recommends fiscal plans and policies for consideration in the Finance Committee; promotes people’s participation in development planning; conducts continuing studies researches and training programs necessary to evolve plans and programs for implementation; exercises supervision over the Secretariat of the MDC; and exercises such other powers and performs such other duties and functions as may be prescribed by law or ordinance.

Office of the Municipal Engineer

This Office is manned by the Municipal Engineer assisted by an Engineer III.

The Municipal Engineer initiates, reviews and recommends changes in policies and objectives, plans and programs, techniques, procedures and practices in infrastructure development and public works in general of the Local Government Unit. They are also in charged in advising the Mayor about infrastructure and public works; administers, coordinates, supervises and controls the construction, maintenance, improvement and repair of roads, bridges and other engineering and public works projects of the Local Government Unit; provides engineering services to the LGU, including investigation and survey, engineering designs, feasibility studies and project management and exercises such other powers and performs such other duties and functions as may be prescribed by law or ordinance.

Office of the Municipal Civil Registrar

This office is headed by the Municipal Civil Registrar, supported by the Registration Officer I and the Assistant Registration Officer.

The Municipal Civil Registrar (MCR) is in-charge of the Civil Registry Office and performs the duties and responsibilities in connection with civil registration and those which is provided for under the New Local Government Code, which includes developing plans and strategies on civil registry programs; accepts documents and judicial decrees affecting the civil status of persons; files and preserves in a secure place books required by law; transcribes and enters immediately upon receipt of documents in the appropriate civil registry books; transmits to the Office of the Civil Registrar-General within the prescribed period, duplicate copies of registered documents required by law; issues certified transcripts or copies of any certificate or registered documents upon payment of the prescribed fees to the Treasurer; receives applications for the issuance of a marriage license and after determining that the requirements and supporting certificates and publication thereof for the prescribed period have been complied with, issues the license upon payment of the authorized fee to the Treasurer; coordinates with the National Statistics Office (NSO) in conducting educational campaigns for vital registration and assists in the presentation of demographic and other statistics for the Local Government Unit; and exercises such powers and performs such other duties and functions as may be prescribed by law or ordinance.

Office of the Municipal Health Officer

This office is under the supervision of the Municipal Health Officer. The office is composed of the personnel in the Medical Section, Population Program Section and Environment Sanitation Section.

There are seven (7) personnel under the Municipal Health Officer, all of them are organic.

The Municipal Health Officer (MHO) is in-charge of all concerns regarding health care and services as well as the supervision of the personnel and staff of the office. The MHO also formulates program implementation guidelines and rules and regulations for the operation of the said office for the approval of the Mayor in order to assist him in the efficient, effective and economical implementation of health services program geared to implementation related projects and activities; formulates measures for the consideration of the Sangguniang Bayan and provides technical assistance and support to the Mayor in carrying out activities to ensure the delivery of basic services and provision of adequate health facilities relative to health services; develops plans and strategies on health programs and projects, and implements them upon approval thereof by the Mayor; formulates and implements policies, plans programs and projects to promote the health of the people; advices the Mayor and the Sangguniang Bayan on matters pertaining to health; executes and enforces all laws, ordinances and regulations relating to public health; recommends to the SB through the Health Board, the passage of ordinances deemed necessary for the preservation of public health; recommends the prosecution of any violation of sanitary laws, ordinances or regulations; direct the Sanitation Inspector to inspect all business establishments selling food items or providing accommodations in accordance with the sanitation code; conducts health information campaigns and renders health intelligence services; coordinates with other government agencies and non-government organizations involved in the promotion and delivery of health services; be in the frontline of the delivery of health services, particularly during and in the aftermath of man-made and natural calamities and disasters; exercises such other powers and performs such other duties and functions as may be prescribed by law and ordinance.

Office of the Municipal Agriculturist

This office is under the guidance of the Municipal Agriculturist with five (5) Agricultural Technologists assisting.

The Municipal Agriculturist is responsible in the delivery of the agricultural services and technical assistance to the constituents of the municipality. The office performs duties and functions as provided for under the New Local Government Code, including the formulation of measures for the approval of the Sangguniang Bayan and provides technical assistance and support to the Mayor in carrying out said measures to ensure the delivery of basic services and provision of adequate facilities relative to agricultural services; develops plans and strategies on agricultural programs and projects and implements them upon approval thereof by the Mayor.

The Office also ensures that maximum assistance and access to resources in the production, processing and marketing of agricultural, aqua-cultural and marine products are extended to farmers, fisherman and local entrepreneurs, conducts or causes to be conducted location-specific agricultural researches and assists in making available the appropriate technology arising out of, and disseminate information on, basic research on crops, prevention and control of plant diseases and pests, and other agricultural matters which will maximize productivity; assists the Mayor in the establishments and extension services of demonstration farms or aquaculture and marine products; enforces rules and regulations relating to agriculture and aquaculture; coordinates with national government agencies and non-government organizations which promote agricultural productivity through appropriate technology compatible with environmental integrity; be in the frontline of delivery of basic agricultural services, particularly those needed for the survival of the inhabitants during and in the aftermath of man-made and natural disasters; recommends to the Sangguniang Bayan and advises the Mayor on all other matters related to agricultural and aquaculture which will improve the livelihood and living conditions of the inhabitants; and exercises such other powers and performs such other duties and functions as may be prescribed by law or ordinance.

Office of the Municipal Social Welfare and Development

This office is headed by the Municipal Social Welfare and Development Officer (MSWDO) and assisted by the Social Welfare Officer II and the Social Welfare Assistant.

The MSWDO is responsible in advising the Mayor for the development and implementation of a comprehensive social welfare program designed to uplift the living conditions and empower the disadvantaged children, youth, women, older persons, person with disabilities, families in crisis and communities needing assistance. This consists of the prevention and remedial programs and services for individuals, families and communities; the protective, remedial and development welfare services for children and the youth; vocational rehabilitation and related services for the physically handicapped. In addition, the MSWDO is also in-charged with establishing programs and policies for the protection of rights of poor and vulnerable sectors and for the immediate relief and early recovery of disaster victims/survivors as well as ensuring the continuing compliance of social welfare development to standards in the delivery of social welfare services; and exercises such other powers and performs such other duties and functions as may be prescribed by law or ordinance.

Office of the Municipal Environment and Natural Resources

This office is manned single-handedly by the Municipal Environment and Natural Resources Officer (MENRO).

The MENRO is responsible for the conservation, management, development and proper use of the municipality’s environment and natural resources, as well as the licensing and regulation of all natural resources utilization as may be provided by law, where applicable. The MENRO also is involved in the supervision and control over forest lands and mineral resources and impose appropriate payments, fees and charges and issues certification upon payment of the prescribed fees to the Treasurer for the exploration, development, utilization or gathering of such resources; establish policies and implement programs for the preservation of cultural and natural heritage through wildlife conservation and segregation of national parks and other protected areas and for the maintenance of a wholesome natural environment by enforcing environmental protection laws; regulate the development, disposition, extraction, exploration and use of land and mineral resources within the municipality; promulgate rules and regulations for the control of water, air and land pollution; and exercise other functions and perform other acts as may be necessary for the protection of the environment.
